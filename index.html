About Me

I'm a U.S. Coast Guard veteran and near-licensed Journeyman Electrician with 7,700 hours in commercial and residential electrical work across North Alabama. I've diagnosed and repaired a shipboard SPS-73 surface search radar, maintained navigation systems on 378-foot High Endurance Cutters, and built a 3D printer from raw parts — rebuilding it four times until it ran right.


I hold a B.A. in Game Art & Development from SNHU (4.0 GPA, Summa Cum Laude) and build software on the side — from gamified budget apps to interactive Phaser art experiences. I'm drawn to work that lives at the intersection of electricity, craft, and creativity.

Creative Vision

Electricity isn't just wiring — it's the medium. I want to bring the same hands-on rigor I apply to commercial electrical work into objects that people can see, feel, and interact with.

From custom lamp design to small kinetic and moving art pieces, I'm building toward a practice that fuses technical craft with creative intent. The sketches are underway. The skills are already there.

If you're a designer, gallery, or individual looking for custom electrical art commissions — I'd love to talk.
